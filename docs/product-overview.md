# Product Overview

## Purpose

MindMap Care is an explainable AI screening and triage platform for mental health, cognitive, and functional risk stratification in African health systems.

The product helps frontline teams capture structured screening information, generate a transparent risk category, and move patients into a clear review, referral, or follow-up workflow. It is intended to support clinical and programme decision-making, not replace it.

## Product Positioning

MindMap Care is a screening and triage support tool. It is not a diagnostic system, emergency response service, treatment protocol, or electronic medical record.

The MVP should help answer:

- Who has been screened?
- Which cases appear to need review first?
- Why was a case placed in a risk category?
- What action should the care team consider next?
- Which referrals or follow-ups are pending?
- Where are programme bottlenecks emerging?

## Primary Users

- Nurses, clinical officers, and primary care clinicians
- Community health workers and outreach teams
- Mental health coordinators and care navigators
- Facility managers and programme leads
- Monitoring, evaluation, and implementation teams
- Research and validation partners

## Core MVP Capabilities

- Structured screening intake form
- Mock risk scoring and triage category generation
- Patient risk summary with explanation and audit metadata
- Triage queue for review prioritization
- Dashboard overview for facility or programme operations
- Backend API contracts for screening, scoring, dashboard summaries, and risk summaries
- Internal documentation for validation, safety, and pilot readiness

## What The Product Does Not Do

MindMap Care does not:

- Diagnose depression, dementia, disability, neurological disease, or any other condition
- Make autonomous clinical decisions
- Replace clinician review
- Provide emergency crisis management by itself
- Claim clinical validity before local validation
- Store real patient data in this repository

## Trust Requirements

Clinicians and pilot partners need to trust the product because it is clear about:

- Intended use and non-use
- Input data used for triage
- Explanation of risk outputs
- Model limitations
- Human accountability
- Data protection and auditability
- Validation status

The product should be judged by workflow usefulness, safety, transparency, and local validation readiness before any claim of clinical impact.
